# Nigeria Macroeconomic Business Insights

## 1. The Oil Boom Peak (1969-1970)
Nigeria hit its highest ever GDP growth of 25% in 1970 — post civil war reconstruction + oil price surge. This represents Nigeria's greatest economic achievement and has never been replicated.

## 2. The Stagflation Trap (1993-1995)
GDP was negative or near zero while inflation hit 72.8% in 1995. Classic stagflation driven by political instability following the annulment of the 1993 elections. Nigeria's worst economic period in modern history.

## 3. Longest Growth Streak (2000-2014)
14 consecutive years of positive GDP growth averaging 6.5% — driven by Paris Club debt cancellation and oil revenue. Nigeria's most stable modern growth era.

## 4. Strong Growth Dominates But Inflation Never Rests
Nigeria spent 26 out of 63 years in Strong Growth — but inflation was rarely under control during those same years, meaning growth rarely translated to improved purchasing power.

## 5. The Double Shock (2016 & 2020)
Two recessions within 4 years — oil crash then COVID-19. Nigeria became one of the few countries to experience back-to-back recessions in the modern era.

## 6. Black Market Premium Reached 85% in 2022
CBN Rate: 440.60 vs Black Market: 817.93 — a gap of 377.33 naira (85.64% premium). This dual exchange rate system creates massive arbitrage and capital flight.
